Consumer Price Index In Australia

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is a measure that tracks the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for a basket of goods and services. It reflects inflation and provides insight into the cost of living. In Australia, the rising cost of living has been driven by factors such as housing, rent, groceries, and transportation. Increasing CPI values indicate that households are facing higher expenses for basic necessities.

The purpose of this visualisation is to explore the consumer price index in Australia as well as compare it to other countries.

Worldwide Consumer Price Index Overview

Australia's Consumer Price Index is relatively lower compared to many other parts of the world. This suggests that while inflation is present, the increase in the cost of goods and services in Australia is less severe than in other countries. A lower CPI can indicate a more stable economy, where price increases are more moderate, and consumers may face fewer immediate financial pressures compared to regions with higher inflation rates.

Quarterly CPI Changes in Cost of Living

This data outlines the CPI changes across different sectors in Australia for the previous quarter. The largest increase was seen in clothing and footwear, which rose by 3.1%, while alcohol and tobacco and health saw increases of 1.5% each. Food and non-alcoholic beverages and insurance and financial services also experienced a rise of 1.2%. Communication was the only category to see a decrease, with a drop of 0.8%, while other sectors like housing and transport showed more moderate increases.

CPI Variations: Goods and Services Comparison

This data presents the CPI changes for goods and services in Australia from 2016 to 2024. Over this period, goods experienced significant fluctuations, peaking at 8.4% in 2022, before declining to 3.2% in 2024. Services, after a dip in 2020 (-2.2%), saw a sharp rise, reaching a high of 6.3% in 2023, and settling at 4.5% in 2024. The data highlights the variability in inflation rates for both goods and services, with notable spikes in recent years.

Annual CPI Changes in Grocery Products

This data shows the CPI percentage changes for various grocery categories in Australia. Non-alcoholic beverages had the highest increase at 4.2%, followed by food products n.e.c (4%) and bread and cereal products (3.9%). Meat and seafoods was the only category that saw a decline, with a -0.4% change. Overall, the data reflects rising costs across most grocery items, indicating inflationary pressures on everyday essentials.
